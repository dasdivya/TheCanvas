ABOUT

ADHD is a common behavioral disorder. Those with ADHD act without thinking, are
hyperactive, and have trouble focusing. They may understand what's expected of them but have
trouble following through because they can't sit still, pay attention, or focus on details.

The left and right hemispheres of our brain process information in different ways. While we
have a natural tendency towards one way of thinking, the two sides of our brain work together
in our everyday lives. The right brain of the brain focuses on the visual, and processes
information in an intuitive and simultaneous way, looking first at the whole picture then the
details. The focus of the left brain is verbal, processing information in an analytical and
sequential way, looking first at the pieces then putting them together to get the whole.

Canvas is a way to help ADHD, and the boredom caused by it, by stimulating the Right Brain.
Canvas use color and motion to create a sort of novelty. The brain appreciates novelty and as
a response to that appreciation generates dopamine which in turn keep the mind intrigued,
happy, attentive and awake. This helps one focus while still giving them a chance to take a
break from assignments or work.

Instead of watching television, playing computer games or engaging in any other kind of
unproductive activity, Canvas provides an easy escape for those who are easily distracted
and/or suffer from ADHD. It is not only easy to use but also provides a creative outlet. It
supplies a new visual almost every time the page is refreshed by generating a randomised
color palette to work with.

Enjoy playing productively!
